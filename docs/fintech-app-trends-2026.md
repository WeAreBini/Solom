# Fintech App Trends 2026: Research Report

**Research Date:** March 1, 2026  
**Prepared for:** Solom Finance Platform  
**Source:** CB Insights, Industry Reports, Market Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

The fintech landscape in 2026 is characterized by three dominant forces: **AI Agent Integration**, **Institutional Digital Asset Adoption**, and **B2B Fintech Momentum**. For Solom, this represents significant opportunities to differentiate through AI-powered features, real-time market intelligence, and seamless wealth management experiences.

---

## Top 10 Fintech Trends for 2026

### 1. AI Agents Transform Financial Services

**What's happening:** AI agents are reshaping how financial work gets done, from automated trading to personalized financial advice.

**Key Signals:**
- CB Insights webinar: "AI Agents Driving ROI: Real-world use cases in action"
- Report: "100 real-world applications of genAI across financial services and insurance"
- Banks investing heavily in "AI readiness" metrics

**Implications for Solom:**
- ✅ Implement AI-powered portfolio analysis
- ✅ Add intelligent alerts and anomaly detection
- ✅ Create conversational interface for market queries
- ✅ Build predictive analytics for stock movements

---

### 2. Institutional DeFi & Digital Assets

**What's happening:** Traditional financial institutions are embracing blockchain and digital assets as core infrastructure.

**Key Signals:**
- CB Insights: "Banking on Digital Assets: How Traditional Finance is Investing in Blockchain"
- State of Fintech 2025: "Institutional DeFi" identified as major trend
- Stablecoins becoming the new M&A battleground

**Implications for Solom:**
- Consider cryptocurrency portfolio tracking
- Support for stablecoin transaction monitoring
- Integration with crypto exchanges for unified view
- Educational content on digital asset investing

---

### 3. Prediction Markets Expansion

**What's happening:** Prediction markets are moving from niche to mainstream, with applications beyond betting.

**Key Signals:**
- State of Fintech 2025: Prediction markets expansion highlighted
- Growing interest in event-based financial instruments

**Implications for Solom:**
- Could explore prediction market data as sentiment indicators
- Display relevant prediction market odds alongside stock data
- Consider social sentiment features

---

### 4. Wealth Tech Innovation

**What's happening:** Wealth management is being democratized through technology, with focus on retail investors.

**Key Signals:**
- Fintech 100 2025 includes wealth management companies
- State of Fintech reports highlight wealth tech growth
- SMB fintech market map shows 105+ companies serving smaller players

**Implications for Solom:**
- ✅ Core focus area - continue building wealth management features
- Portfolio analytics and performance tracking
- Goal-based investing tools
- Tax optimization features

---

### 5. B2B Fintech Momentum

**What's happening:** Business-to-business fintech is seeing strong funding and adoption.

**Key Signals:**
- State of Fintech Q2'25: "B2B momentum" noted
- SMB fintech market map: 105+ companies in this space
- Cross-border payments infrastructure investment

**Implications for Solom:**
- Consider B2B features for financial advisors
- Partner platform for institutional clients
- API access for third-party integrations

---

### 6. Crypto Payments Infrastructure

**What's happening:** Cryptocurrency payment rails are maturing as enterprise infrastructure.

**Key Signals:**
- Fintech 100 2025: "crypto payments infrastructure" category
- Stablecoin M&A activity
- Cross-border payment innovations

**Implications for Solom:**
- Support for crypto asset tracking
- Integration with crypto payment providers
- Display crypto alongside traditional assets

---

### 7. AI Readiness Benchmarking

**What's happening:** Banks and fintechs are competing on AI capabilities, measured by partnerships, patents, and talent.

**Key Signals:**
- CB Insights: "Retail banking AI readiness" report
- AI becoming a competitive differentiator
- Focus on deploying, securing, and scaling AI systems

**Implications for Solom:**
- Invest in AI infrastructure early
- Build ML models for personalization
- Document AI capabilities for competitive positioning

---

### 8. Regulatory & Compliance Tech (RegTech)

**What's happening:** Compliance automation is critical as financial regulations evolve.

**Key Signals:**
- Regulatory technology growing as a category
- KYC/AML automation becoming standard
- Real-time compliance monitoring

**Implications for Solom:**
- Implement robust KYC processes
- Automated compliance reporting
- Privacy-by-design architecture

---

### 9. Embedded Finance Expansion

**What's happening:** Financial services are being embedded into non-financial products.

**Key Signals:**
- Big Tech (Amazon, Google) embedding into financial ecosystem
- APIs enabling embedded lending, payments, insurance
- Companies "becoming fintechs" without being fintechs

**Implications for Solom:**
- Consider API-first architecture for embedded use
- White-label opportunities
- Partnership integrations

---

### 10. Mega-Rounds & Market Consolidation

**What's happening:** While overall deal volume decreased, mega-rounds ($100M+) increased, indicating market maturation.

**Key Signals:**
- US-based mega-rounds surge in 2025
- M&A gaining steam
- Investors favor "measurable commercial traction" over speculation

**Implications for Solom:**
- Focus on revenue metrics and user growth
- Build sustainable competitive advantages
- Prepare for potential acquisition interest

## Key Metrics to Track

Based on industry research, track these metrics for Solom:

### Engagement
- Daily/Monthly Active Users (DAU/MAU)
- Session duration
- Features used per session
- Alert interaction rate

### Retention
- 7-day, 30-day, 90-day retention
- Cohort analysis
- Feature retention (which features keep users)

### Revenue (when applicable)
- Revenue per user
- Conversion rate (free to paid)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Lifetime value (LTV)

### Product Health
- API latency (target < 100ms)
- Data freshness (real-time vs delayed)
- Error rates
- Feature adoption rates
