## Executive Summary

The fintech landscape in 2026 represents a pivotal transformation year. After years of regulatory uncertainty and market volatility, the industry is experiencing renewed clarity and confidence. AI has evolved from novelty to necessity, stablecoins are entering mainstream infrastructure, and embedded finance is becoming a structural feature of digital platforms.

**Key Finding:** Fintech in 2026 is no longer about individual apps or niche services—it's a fundamental reshaping of the financial landscape where cloud-native systems, modular architectures, AI agents, tokenized assets, and real-time payment rails are becoming the backbone of modern finance.

## Top 10 Fintech Trends for 2026

### 1. AI as Financial Co-Pilot

**The Shift:** Consumers no longer see fintech apps as standalone tools—they expect apps to help them understand finances and make smarter decisions.

**Key Developments:**
- **57% of consumers** now expect their fintech apps to use AI
- AI is becoming the layer that helps people decide what to do before they take action
- The opportunity gap: 81% want financial education, only 19% receive it from their apps

**Implications for Solom:**
- Integrate AI-powered insights for stock analysis and portfolio recommendations
- Develop educational features that guide users through financial decisions
- Create proactive notifications that help users understand market movements

**Sources:** Plaid Fintech Trends 2026, Plaid Fintech Effect Report 2025

---

### 2. Agentic AI Transforms Payments

**The Shift:** AI is moving from analytics to autonomous execution—planning and executing multi-step actions without explicit step-by-step instructions.

**Key Developments:**
- **43% of banks** deploying AI in internal functions (risk, compliance, fraud)
- Only 9% using AI in customer-facing channels (opportunity ahead)
- Goldman Sachs launched firmwide AI assistant for thousands of employees

**Agentic AI Capabilities in 2026:**
- Plan and execute online transactions end-to-end (discovery to checkout)
- Monitor subscription renewal risk
- Identify upcoming bill payments across accounts
- Negotiate small incentives for pending transactions
- Process applications and flag compliance risks

**Requirements for Adoption:**
- Clean API layers
- Unified payment metadata
- Explainable decision logs for audit trails
- Human approval checkpoints
- Guardrails against misaligned agent behavior

**Implications for Solom:**
- Build AI-ready infrastructure with clean APIs
- Consider agentic features for automated portfolio rebalancing
- Develop explainable AI for trade recommendations

**Sources:** BDO 2026 Fintech Predictions, Trinetix, S&P Global

---

### 3. Fraud Prevention Becomes Network-Based

**The Shift:** Traditional fraud prevention is no longer sufficient. Bad actors operate across multiple platforms, requiring a coordinated defense.

**Key Statistics:**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US Fraud Losses (2023) | $12.3 billion |
| Projected AI-Driven Fraud Losses (2027) | $40 billion |
| US Fraud Losses (2024) | $12.5 billion |
| Financial Services Share of AI Cyberattacks (2025) | 33% |

**Key Developments:**
- Fraudsters use AI to scale attacks—deepfakes and synthetic identities
- J.P. Morgan and Wells Fargo embedded LLMs in payment screening
- Network-level detection spotting fraud rings across institutions
- Fraud moves upstream—detecting identity issues before money moves

**Consumer Expectations:**
- 80% want instant breach notifications
- 80% want transparency on personal data use
- 80% want aggressive fraud protections

**Implications for Solom:**
- Implement real-time fraud detection with AI
- Partner with network-level fraud platforms (like Plaid Protect)
- Build user trust through transparent security practices
- Implement adaptive, multi-factor authentication

**Sources:** Plaid, FTC, Deloitte

### 4. Alternative Payments Go Mainstream

**The Shift:** New payment types are rapidly gaining adoption, challenging traditional card networks.

**Key Metrics:**
| Payment Type | Metric |
|--------------|--------|
| P2P Bank Payments (US, 2026) | ~184 million mobile users |
| Pay-by-Bank Share | 1.5% of all consumer transactions |
| RTP Network Volume Growth (Q4 2024-Q4 2025) | 28% increase |
| RTP Network Value Growth (Q4 2024-Q4 2025) | 405% increase |

**Infrastructure Developments:**
- **FedNow** and **RTP** adoption accelerating real-time bank-to-bank payments
- **Request for Payment (RfP)** allows businesses to send payment requests directly to consumer banking apps
- Cross-border payments becoming instant and low-cost via stablecoins

**Implications for Solom:**
- Integrate real-time payment options for premium features
- Consider pay-by-bank for subscription payments (lower fees)
- Explore instant settlement for trading features

**Sources:** Plaid, Insider Intelligence, eMarketer, The Clearing House

### 5. Lending Moves Beyond Credit Scores

**The Shift:** Traditional credit scores reflect historical data, not current financial reality. Alternative data is expanding access.

**Key Statistics:**
- **49 million Americans** lack access to loans due to traditional scoring limitations
- Lenders combining cash flow data, pay stubs, utility bills with credit scores
- API-based tools and open banking enabling instant alternative data access

**New Lending Data Sources:**
- Real-time cash flow analysis
- Income verification via payroll APIs
- Utility and rent payment history
- Behavioral financial patterns

**Implications for Solom:**
- If offering credit features, integrate alternative data sources
- Consider partnerships with cash-flow underwriting providers
- Financial education features can improve user creditworthiness

**Sources:** Plaid

---

### 6. Open Banking Becomes Baseline Expectation

**The Shift:** Open banking is no longer a differentiator—it's a requirement for customer acquisition and retention.

**Consumer Expectations:**
- 77% say their bank MUST connect to apps they use
- 72% say connectivity is a top priority when choosing a bank
- 66% would consider switching banks over connectivity issues
- 70%+ only trust banks that connect with fintech apps

**Implications for Solom:**
- Prioritize bank connectivity integrations (Plaid, Yodlee, etc.)
- Make account aggregation seamless
- Ensure reliable data syncing across all supported institutions

**Sources:** Plaid Fintech Effect Report 2025

### 7. Stablecoins Enter Mainstream Infrastructure

**The Shift:** Stablecoins are no longer niche—they're becoming foundational payment infrastructure.

**Key Statistics:**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Stablecoin Trading Volume (2024) | $23 trillion |
| YoY Growth | 90% |
| Neobanks Launching on Stablecoin Rails | Increasing trend |

**Regulatory Framework:**
- **GENIUS Act** (July 2025): First comprehensive US stablecoin regulatory framework
- Requires federal/state regulatory supervision
- 100% reserve banking with liquid assets
- Mandatory AML programs

**Use Cases:**
- Neobanks building directly on stablecoin rails
- Cross-border payments at fraction of traditional costs
- Instant settlement without traditional banking delays

**Challenges:**
- Regulatory fragmentation across jurisdictions
- AML/KYC compliance complexity
- Money laundering and sanctions evasion risks
- Consumer trust and dispute resolution

**Implications for Solom:**
- Monitor stablecoin infrastructure opportunities
- Consider stablecoin settlement for cross-border features
- Prepare for regulatory compliance requirements

**Sources:** IMF, BDO, Taylor Wessing

### 9. Tokenization of Real-World Assets

**The Shift:** Fractional ownership of high-value assets is becoming mainstream through blockchain tokenization.

**Asset Classes Being Tokenized:**
- Real estate (fractional ownership from $1,000)
- Commodities
- Agriculture
- Private equity funds
- Art and collectibles

**Regulatory Progress:**
- EU DLT Pilot Regime amendments proposed
- Traditional banks introducing tokenization in 2025
- Regulatory clarity still needed for widespread adoption

**Barriers:**
- Regulatory uncertainty around token classification
- Public skepticism requiring mainstream adoption to overcome
- Legal frameworks for smart contract-based ownership

**Implications for Solom:**
- Monitor tokenized securities opportunities for trading platform
- Consider fractional investment features
- Stay informed on tokenized asset regulations

**Sources:** BDO, Taylor Wessing

- Clearer path for crypto/digital asset features
